FIRST STEP

Obtain registration in your locality.

- You must request it at the town hall of your locality. If you are already registered in the locality where you live, proceed to step 3.

(If you want to register in Palma, you must request an appointment here).

- When you attend your appointment, you must bring this document filled out along with your passport (plus a copy) and your rental contract (plus a copy).

- Request a certificate of registration, you will need it in the immigration office.


SECOND STEP

Submit all documentation to the immigration office.

- Request an appointment at the immigration office here.

- Documents to present: Model X18, Fee and the certificate from the first step.

DEPENDING ON THE CASE:

CASE 1:

If you have been an employee, you can provide any of the following documents:

- Employment contract registered in the Public Employment Service.

- Report from the working life report or consent for checking this data in the file of the Social Security Treasury.

If you are a self-employed worker, you can provide any of the following documents:

- Continuous registration in the Census of Economic Activities.

- Continuous registration in the Commercial Registry.

- Document of enrollment or a situation similar to enrollment in the corresponding Social Security regime, or consent for checking this data in the files of the Social Security Treasury or the Tax Agency.

If you do not work in Spain, you must provide:

- Proof of holding a public or private health insurance, or if you are a pensioner, a certification of having received health care funded by the state for which you receive a pension.

- Documentation proving sufficient resources for yourself and your family during the period of residence in Spain.

If you are a student, you must provide:

- Enrollment at a public or private educational institution recognized or funded by the competent education administration.

- Documentation proving access to public or private health insurance. You can provide a European Health Insurance Card with a validity covering the residency period and that has enabled you to receive necessary health benefits.

- A responsible declaration of having sufficient resources for yourself and your family members during your residency period in Spain.

If you are a family member of a Union citizen, you must provide:

- Documentation proving the family relationship with the Union citizen.

- If applicable, documentation proving that you are financially dependent on the Union citizen.

- Registration certificate of the accompanying family member.

- Documentation proving that the entitled family member has been a worker (self-employed or employee) for five years, or has had sufficient means of support and health insurance, or has been a student with sufficient means for themselves and their family members, along with health insurance.


CASE 2:

- Documentation proving having been an employed or self-employed worker and having ceased activity to access retirement with the right to a pension.


CASE 3:

- Documentation proving access to early retirement.

- Documentation proving having worked in Spain for at least twelve months.

In case you have not resided in Spain for more than three years, documentation proving marriage or being a registered partner of a Spanish citizen, or being a citizen who lost Spanish nationality after marriage or registration as a partner with the worker.


CASE 4:

- Documentation proving ceasing activity and being permanently incapacitated.

In case you have not resided in Spain for two years continuously, documentation proving that the incapacity is a result of a work accident or occupational disease with a pension right by a Spanish State agency, or documentation proving marriage or being a partner of a Spanish citizen or a citizen who lost Spanish nationality after marriage or registration as a partner with the worker.


CASE 5:

- Documentation proving performing your activity in another member state and returning to Spanish territory daily or at least once a week.


CASE 6:

- Documentation proving the family relationship with the Union citizen.

If applicable, documentation proving that you are financially dependent on the Union citizen and that there is a real dependency situation of dependents over 21 years old. Important note: when submitting documents from other countries, they must be translated into Spanish or the co-official language of the territory where the application is submitted. When they are standard forms of the European Union, their translation or legalization will not be necessary in accordance with the Community regulations that support them. For example: healthcare models S1, E109, E121...


Every foreign public document must be previously legalized by the Spanish consular office with jurisdiction in the country where the document was issued or, where appropriate, by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Cooperation, except in cases where the document has been apostilled by the competent authority of the issuing country according to the Hague Convention of October 5, 1961, and unless said document is exempt from legalization under an International Agreement. For more information on the translation and legalization of documents, please consult information sheet 108.
